
MEDICAID ELIGIBILITY & COVERAGE POLICY

1. General Eligibility
Applicants must meet one or more of the following criteria:
- Low income individuals
- Pregnant individuals
- Children under the Childrenâ€™s Health Insurance Program (CHIP)
- Seniors or persons with disabilities

2. Income Requirements
Most states use the Federal Poverty Level (FPL) as the benchmark.
For adults, eligibility generally requires income to be under $18,000 per year.
Some states may adjust this threshold based on household size.

3. Covered Benefits
Medicaid covers a wide range of essential services, including:
- Primary care visits
- Hospitalization and emergency services
- Prescription medications
- Mental health and substance-use treatment
- Preventive care and screenings

4. Prior Authorization Requirements
Certain procedures require prior approval:
- MRI and CT scans
- High-cost medications
- Non-emergency surgeries

5. Exclusions
Medicaid does not cover:
- Cosmetic procedures
- Experimental treatments
- Services not deemed medically necessary

6. Renewal & Documentation
Eligibility must be renewed annually.
Applicants must provide income verification and proof of residency.
